# Project Vision

## Why This Project Exists
Financial literacy is not universally taught, yet it universally impacts human well-being. In India, millions of young adults make critical life choices—taking out high-interest loans, navigating complex tax regimes, investing for retirement without understanding inflation—without fully grasping the long-term compounding effects of those decisions.

The cost of learning through real-world mistakes is often decades of financial hardship. Passive education (like textbooks and lectures) often fails to change actual financial behavior.

## Our Approach
We believe the most effective way to learn is through experience. By creating an interactive, realistic life simulator, we can provide a safe sandbox for citizens to experiment with financial strategies. 

Users can experience 50 years of financial decisions in 30 minutes. They can see what happens if they invest early via SIPs, take on predatory debt, or neglect to buy health insurance before a medical emergency. 

## The Ultimate Goal
To become the national standard for accessible, open-source financial education in India. 

We envision this tool being adopted as a **Digital Public Good (DPG)** by organizations like the National Centre for Financial Education (NCFE), used by teachers in classrooms, and leveraged by NGOs aiming to uplift communities. By open-sourcing the engine, we ensure it remains free, transparent, and constantly updated by India's best developers and financial minds.
