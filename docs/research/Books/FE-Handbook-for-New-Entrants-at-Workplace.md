## 1. Introduction

The most important factor that determines the productivity of an organisation is its Human Resource. An organization may have a mixed-bag of employees. Certainly, it is the employees' hard work and dedication that ultimately improves the wellbeing of a company. If employees are healthy, happy and eager to work, that induces positive change in the organization's fortunes. On the other hand, stressed and anxious employees may have a negative impact. One of the most important reasons for stress among employees is worry about personal finance. A majority of people suffer from money-related problems as their financial management skills are not sound. It is recommended to introduce financial literacy programmes in the workplace so employees understand the basics of financial literacy and manage their money better.

Once employees are financially literate, they can manage their own money much better, and their stress levels over financial issues eventually reduce. This leads to healthier and happier individuals who contribute immensely to the growth of the organisation.

### Importance of Financial Literacy

Financial literacy encompasses knowledge, attitude and behaviour covering money management, planning for short, medium and long-term financial goals, and awareness and choice of financial products.

**Financial Knowledge** → **Financial Behaviour** → **Financial Attitude** = **Financial Literacy**

- **Financial Knowledge:** Understanding key financial concepts and the ability to evaluate benefits in real-life financial situations — simple interest, compound interest, time value of money, inflation, diversification, division, risk-return and interest paid on loans.
- **Financial Behaviour:** Day-to-day money management, financial planning, spending, savings, investment, reliance on credit, and building a safety net for future well-being.
- **Financial Attitude:** People's response toward savings, prioritization of short-term wants over long-term security, inclination towards risk, etc.

Financial literacy is an essential life skill — it equips people to manage money effectively, make sound financial decisions, achieve financial stability, and become debt-free by adopting the best personal finance strategies.

### Why Learn Basic Financial Literacy Concepts?

We live in a world where instant gratification is the norm. Learning takes time, but not learning the basics of financial education increases the chance of costly mistakes. Four reasons to be financially literate:

1. **To Be Financially Literate** — India's literacy rate is 75%, but financial literacy is only 27%. With the world's highest population, citizens need to make informed financial decisions across banking, insurance, mutual funds, stocks, pension, etc.
2. **To Live Within Your Means** — We all dream of a comfortable life, but problems start when we want all our dreams to become reality today, within our current income. Living within your means and weighing pros/cons of every decision is important; otherwise, emotional spending forces borrowing and creates future liability.
   > Warren Buffett: *"If you buy things you do not need, soon you will have to sell things you need."*
3. **To Save and Invest** — Earning money alone cannot solve problems; money must grow and beat inflation. Personal finance knowledge helps decide how much to save, which investment options to consider, and how to look at post-tax, post-inflation returns.
4. **To Understand Taxes** — Most people are unaware of deductions and exemptions under the Income Tax Act. For example, Section 80C allows lowering taxable income by up to ₹1.5 lakh; Section 80CCD allows an additional ₹50,000 investment in NPS for tax deduction.

## 2. Basic Financial Literacy Concepts

### Personal Financial Literacy

**Income, Expenses and Budgeting**

**Income:** Most people have income through job, business, or farming, and possibly interest income from investments. You need to track and manage it to cover expenses and save for the future.

**Expenses:** It costs money to live — food, clothing, housing, transportation, communication, and more, plus discretionary spending like vacations and entertainment. Two things to do:
1. Know what your expenses are
2. Reduce unnecessary spending

Track expenses using this habit:
- Keep every receipt
- Record every expense daily
- Total your expenses at month end
- Do this for 3 months

**Budgeting:** A budget is simply a comparison of income and expenses.
- If **positive** → surplus: pay off debt or increase savings/investment.
- If **negative** → deficit: reduce expenses or increase income.

Budgeting is not a one-time thing — do it regularly (weekly at first, then monthly/yearly).

**Saving:** Savings is the portion of income not spent on current expenditure.

> Saving = Income − Expenditure ❌ (wrong framing)
> Expenditure = Income − Saving ✅ (correct framing — set aside savings first)

**Why to Save?**
- Provides greater security in life (future goals: bike, home, marriage, child education).
- Prepares you for unexpected events/emergencies.
- Enables you to take risks to try new things.

**How to Save — Points to Keep in Mind:**
- Diversify your savings across instruments.
- Keep some portion in liquid assets for withdrawal when needed.
- Avoid very risky/unregulated instruments — you may lose everything.

**General Tips for Saving:**
- Make regular contributions (automatic monthly transfer).
- Plan your saving and spending; reduce unnecessary expenses.
- Pay yourself first — set aside savings before spending; save a portion of any income increase.
- Clear high-interest debts before starting savings.
- Use tax benefit schemes (EPF, PPF, NSC, ELSS, SSY, NPS) to maximize savings.

### Needs and Wants

A **need** is something you must have (food, water, shelter, clothing, medications). A **want** is something that makes life more enjoyable/comfortable but you could do without (eating out, movies, expensive clothes/jewellery). One person's want may be another's need (e.g., a costly cricket bat is a need for a school team player).

### Short, Medium and Long-Term Goals

- **Short-Term (6 months–1 year):** Rent payments, clearing credit/debit card bills, buying a smartphone.
- **Medium-Term (1–7 years):** Buying a car, paying off education loans, planning a foreign trip.
- **Long-Term (7–25 years or till retirement):** Retirement planning, buying a house, children's education & marriage.

### Power of Compounding

With simple interest, you earn interest only on the principal. With compound interest, you earn interest on principal plus previously earned interest.
Example: ₹100 invested for 10 years at 10% → ₹200 (simple interest) vs ₹260 approx. (compound interest).

### Setting SMART Goals

**S.M.A.R.T** = Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Realistic, Time-bound.

Example: "Saving 50,000 rupees for a 100cc motorbike within 10 months" is SMART — specific, measurable, achievable/realistic (₹5,000/month), and time-bound (10 months).

> "Do not save what is left after spending, but spend what is left after saving." — Warren Buffett

## 3. Banking & Finance

A bank is a financial institution that accepts deposits from the public and creates credit. In India, banking is regulated by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI).

### How to Select the Right Type of Bank

1. **Location of Branch** — near your residence/office.
2. **ATM** — availability in your vicinity.
3. **Average Balance Requirement** — prefer banks with least minimum balance criteria.
4. **Online Banking** — should be safe and available 24/7/365.
5. **Interest Rates** — compare savings account interest rates across banks.
6. **Services and Fees** — prefer banks charging the least for services.

### Types of Bank Deposits

- **Savings Account (Demand Deposit):** Handy for short-term savings, pays nominal interest, money accessible anytime.
- **Recurring Deposits (RD) (Time Deposit):** Build a fund via periodic small monthly savings; no withdrawals before maturity.
- **Fixed Deposits (FD) (Time Deposit):** Deposit a sum for a fixed period; withdrawal before maturity attracts a penalty.

**Auto Sweep Facility:** Combines savings + FD account. When savings balance crosses a defined limit, excess is automatically transferred to FD, earning higher interest than a plain savings account.

### Deposit Insurance

The Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation (DICGC) insures all deposits (savings, fixed, current, recurring) up to a maximum of ₹5,00,000 per depositor per bank (principal + interest). More info: https://www.dicgc.org.in/

### Pradhan Mantri Jan-Dhan Yojana (PMJDY)

A National Mission on Financial Inclusion. Scheme details:
- Basic savings bank account opened for unbanked persons.
- No minimum balance requirement.
- Interest earned on deposits.
- RuPay debit card provided.
- Accident insurance cover of ₹1 lakh (₹2 lakh for accounts opened after 28.8.2018) with RuPay card.
- Overdraft facility up to ₹10,000 for eligible account holders.
- Eligible for DBT, PMJJBY, PMSBY, APY, MUDRA scheme.

More info: https://pmjdy.gov.in/

### Credit and Debt Management

Borrowing from banks/financial institutions for a house, car, or education is called debt. **Good debt** creates value/wealth; **bad debt** is for something that immediately loses value.

**Debit Card vs Credit Card**

| | Debit Card | Credit Card |
|---|---|---|
| Principle | Buy Now Pay Now | Buy Now Pay Later |
| Spending source | Your own bank balance | Borrowed money up to a credit limit |
| Helps you | Avoid debt, track spending, budget with cash | Build credit, maximise rewards, get purchase protection |

### Loans & Advances

A loan is debt incurred by an individual; the lender advances money in return for agreed terms (finance charges, interest, repayment date, conditions).

**Credit Score:** A 3-digit number (300–900) representing creditworthiness, prepared by 4 credit bureaus in India — CIBIL, Experian, CRIF High Mark, and Equifax. Impacts loan approval, amount sanctioned, and interest rate.

**How to Build a Good Credit Score:**
1. Pay EMIs on time.
2. Do not apply for multiple loans at a time.
3. Review your credit score periodically (every 6 months).

A disciplined approach to taking and repaying loans is the main way to build a good credit score.

### Different Types of Loans

- **Personal Loan:** For any personal need; no collateral, higher interest rates.
- **Home Loan:** For constructing, buying land + building, or buying a flat/house. Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY) offers subsidy on first house purchase (https://pmaymis.gov.in).
- **Education Loan:** For higher education; repaid after securing a job. Vidya Lakshmi portal for applying/tracking (https://www.vidyalakshmi.co.in/Students/).
- **Gold Loan:** Short-term loan against gold/gold ornaments as collateral.
- **Vehicle Loan:** Secured loan; bank can repossess vehicle on default.
- **Loan Against Bank FD:** Loan (e.g., ₹80,000 against ₹1,00,000 FD) at a rate higher than the FD's own interest rate.

### Debt Management

Debt management is controlling debt through financial planning and budgeting. Borrowing should be the last resort; avoid loans from unregulated entities.

**Tips to Manage Debt Better:**
1. **Prioritize High-Interest Loans** — list debts by interest rate; focus on credit card/personal loans first (home loans have tax benefits & lower rates).
2. **Consider Loan Consolidation** — use property to pay off high-interest loans; simplify payments.
3. **Boost EMIs with Raises** — increase EMIs with salary hikes/bonuses to accelerate repayment.
4. **Explore Loan Switching** — check for lower rates at other banks, especially for home loans.
5. **Pay Timely to Preserve Credit** — avoid late fees; maintain a good credit history.

**Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana:** A small deposit scheme exclusively for a girl child (part of Beti Bachao Beti Padhao). Meets education/marriage expenses; invest up to ₹1,50,000/year; tax exemption under Section 80C. More: https://www.indiapost.gov.in/Financial/Pages/Content/Post-Office-Saving-Schemes.aspx

## 4. Digital Financial Literacy

Digital payments are payments where both payer and payee use electronic modes.

**Advantages of Digital Payments:**
1. Fast, easy and convenient.
2. Economical with less transaction fee.
3. Provides a digital, trackable record of transactions; one-stop solution for all payment types.

### Methods of Digital Payment

Banking Cards | Bank Prepaid Cards | Internet Banking | Point of Sale | Mobile Banking | USSD | Micro ATMs | AEPS | Mobile Wallets | UPI

**Banking Cards:** Offer more security, convenience and control. Includes credit, debit and prepaid cards with 2-factor authentication (PIN + OTP). Examples: RuPay, Visa, MasterCard.

**Bank Prepaid Cards:** A card loaded with money, used to spend up to that amount (a stored-value card).

**Internet Banking:** Electronic system for conducting financial transactions via a bank's website. Types:
- **NEFT (National Electronic Fund Transfer):** No min/max limit; available 24x7x365.
- **RTGS (Real Time Gross Settlement):** Minimum ₹2,00,000; no maximum limit; real-time high-value transfers; 24x7x365.
- **IMPS (Immediate Payment Service):** Instant transfer via account number/IFSC or MMID; no minimum, maximum ₹5,00,000; 24x7x365.

**Point of Sale (PoS):** The place where a sale/transaction is completed (e.g., checkout counter).

**USSD (*99#):** Mobile banking on basic feature phones without internet data.

**Micro ATMs:** Devices used by Business Correspondents (e.g., local retail shop owners) to deliver basic banking services.

**AEPS (Aadhaar Enabled Payment System):** Bank-led model for online interoperable transactions at PoS/Micro ATM using Aadhaar authentication.

**Mobile Wallet:** Digital cash carried via an app linked to your account (e.g., Paytm, Freecharge, Mobikwik, Airtel Money).

**UPI (Unified Payments Interface):** Merges multiple bank accounts into a single mobile app using a Virtual Payment Address (VPA); 24x7 real-time transfers.
- Benefits: privacy (share only VPA), multiple utility, one-click 2FA (just PIN), works across web/app interfaces.
- More info: https://www.npci.org.in/what-we-do/upi/product-overview

**BHIM:** App for simple UPI-based bank-to-bank payments using mobile number or UPI ID. More info: https://www.bhimupi.org.in/

### Digital Financial Records

Digital financial records are documents available in digital format, accessible on PCs, laptops, and smartphones. Categories include:

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Bank Statements | Crop/Credit Card/Home/Personal/Vehicle Loan Statements |
| Insurance Contracts | Prospectus, Proposal Form, Premium Receipts, Duplicate Policy |
| Investment Statement | PPF, EPF, NPS, Mutual Fund, Stock Broking, Demat, Savings Passbook |
| Tax Records | Income Tax, Property Tax, Sales Tax |
| Property Records | Registration Docs, Sale Deed, NOCs, Encumbrance Certificate, Rent Agreements |
| Vehicle Registration | RC Book, Pollution Certificate, Vehicle Insurance |
| Personal Identity Documents | Aadhar, PAN, Voter ID,Assport, Driving License, Will |

All documents can be stored safely electronically at **DigiLocker** (https://digilocker.gov.in). Many government bodies issue e-documents directly through DigiLocker.

### DigiLocker

A key initiative under Digital India, providing citizens a shareable private space on a public cloud for all documents/certificates. Benefits: access documents anytime/anywhere and share online; government bodies issue e-documents directly through DigiLocker.

## 5. Investment

Investing can help meet financial goals but comes with risk and complexity.

### Key Factors — Return, Risk, Liquidity

1. **Returns:** The profit an investor makes — income or capital form.
2. **Risk:** Uncertainty of returns; risk and return go hand-in-hand — higher returns mean more risk.
3. **Liquidity:** The ability to cash in/sell an investment quickly near market price (e.g., listed stocks, government bonds are liquid).

### Investing Goals by Life Stage

1. **Young:** Willing to take more risk; long time horizon to recover from downturns.
2. **New family:** Need security; balance long-term planning with liquid funds for emergencies/major purchases.
3. **Family becoming independent:** Less need for short-term savings; more for retirement; unwilling to take much risk.
4. **Retired:** Rely on investments for regular reliable income alongside pensions.

### Inflation and Its Effect on Investment

Inflation is a rise in price of goods/services, reducing purchasing power over time. E.g., a Samosa costing ₹5 five years ago now costs ₹10.

**Time Value of Money:** Money available today is worth more than the same amount in the future.

### Diversification

Don't put all your eggs in one basket — spread investments (FDs, Gold, Shares, Mutual Funds) to reduce risk.

### Investment in Securities Market

Securities are broadly classified into two types: **A. Equities** and **B. Debts**.

- **Primary Market:** Where a company issues new stocks/bonds for the first time (e.g., IPO).
- **Secondary Market:** Where securities are traded after issuance, via stock exchanges (BSE Ltd. and NSE in India).

**Know Your Client (KYC):** SEBI-prescribed requirement for all securities market investors; can now be done online via Aadhaar-based e-KYC.

**Equity:** A share/stock of a company. Owning shares means owning part of the company and a share of profits. Riskier and more time-intensive than other investments.

**Debt Securities:** Instruments like bonds, debentures, promissory notes with fixed amount, maturity date, and specific interest rate. Generally less risky than equities.

**Sovereign Gold Bond (SGB):** Government securities denominated in grams of gold, substitutes for physical gold; issued by RBI on behalf of Government of India; redeemed in cash on maturity.

**REITs and InvITs:** Collective Investment Schemes owning real estate/infrastructure sector properties respectively; investors earn dividends without buying/managing properties directly.

### Investment in Government Schemes

National Savings Certificates (NSC) | Post Office Savings/RD Account | Public Provident Fund (PPF) | Kisan Vikas Patra (KVP)

- Recommended for investors seeking safe, fixed returns.
- Interest rates notified quarterly with Ministry of Finance approval.
- Sovereign guarantee on the entire amount invested — highly safe.
- More: https://www.indiapost.gov.in/Financial/Pages/Content/Post-Office-Saving-Schemes.aspx

### Mutual Funds

A mutual fund pools money from many investors and invests in stocks, bonds, money-market instruments, etc. Each unit represents proportionate ownership of the fund's holdings.

- **ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme):** Saves income tax — invest up to ₹1.5 lakh and claim deduction.
- **Gold ETF:** Commodity-based mutual fund investing in gold, traded like stocks on exchanges.
- **SIP (Systematic Investment Plan):** Fixed amount invested at fixed intervals.

**Advantages of SIP:**
1. Power of compounding.
2. Flexibility to choose SIP amount.
3. Inculcates discipline of regular investing ("Good EMI").

More info: https://www.mutualfundssahihai.com/hi

### Stocks

Investing in shares of companies listed on stock exchanges — BSE and NSE in India. Subject to market risk; suitable for investors with adequate fundamental knowledge. Requires a DEMAT account to buy stocks/equities.

### Taking Investment Decisions (4 steps)

1. **Identify Investment Purpose and Needs** — goals should follow SMART guidelines (Emergency Fund, Electronics, Children's Education/Marriage, Home, Car, Vacation, Retirement, etc.).
2. **Prioritize Goal** — limited resources mean not all goals can be pursued simultaneously.
3. **Estimate Amount Required for the Goal** — e.g., a ₹50,000 smartphone.
4. **Estimate the Time When the Goal Will Be Realised** — e.g., within 1 year → invest ₹4,166/month on ₹15,000 salary.

### Factors Affecting Investment Returns

| Factor | Description |
|---|---|
| Risks | Which risk to take and how much to allocate |
| Taxes | Taxability of returns; interest income taxable; LT capital gains on equity tax-free |
| Brokerages & Commissions | Not transparent, often hidden |
| Behaviours & Attitudes | Procrastination, loss aversion, herd mentality, lack of self-control, biases |
| Investing Without Purpose | Leads to wrong risk allocation, distress sales, suboptimal returns |
| Risks (charges) | Demat account charges, locker charges |
| Tracking & Monitoring | Needed for timely course correction; compound vs simple interest awareness |

### Strategies for Investing

- **Take full benefit of Sec 80C** — up to ₹1,50,000 via EPF, PPF, NPS, ELSS, NSC, etc.
- **Take full benefit of Sec 80CCD(1B)** — additional ₹50,000 in NPS.
- **Select pay structure/companies offering 12% EPF contribution** — employer matches, so total becomes 24%.
- **Use bonus/bulk surplus** to pay off loans or invest in SIPs for long-term needs.

## 6. Insurance

1. Life is full of uncertainties.
2. We face various risks in day-to-day life — life, health, property, and more.
3. No one can predict such unforeseen events.
4. Chances may be small, but mishaps do happen.
5. It's only when the event occurs that we realize we should have taken insurance cover.

### Types of Insurance

Motor | Home | Health | Fire | Rural | Personal Accident | Travel | Term

Insurance is a means of protection from financial loss — a shield against contingent/uncertain loss, providing security and peace of mind.

### Need for Insurance

1. Ensure immediate family has financial support in event of death/disability, covering minimum daily expenses.
2. Fulfill and finance children's education and other needs.
3. Ensure loss of income due to serious illness/accident is compensated.

### Key Insurance Types

- **Term Insurance:** Most basic and affordable type of life insurance — pure risk cover with no savings/profits component; sum assured paid to beneficiaries only on death during policy term; premium remains constant throughout the term.
- **Health Insurance:** Covers hospitalization costs or a fixed sum on diagnosis of named critical illnesses. Understand employer-provided group health cover before buying additional cover.
- **Personal Accident Insurance:** Covers risk of death, permanent/temporary total or partial disability due to accident.
- **Vehicle/Motor Insurance:**
  - *Motor Third Party (TP) Liability Insurance* — statutory requirement; covers damage/injury/death of third parties.
  - *Motor Own Damage (OD) Insurance* — covers accidental damage to your own vehicle.
- **Home Loan Insurance:** Ensures EMIs are paid by the insurer to the bank in case of unforeseen death of the loan-taking earning member.
- **Home Insurance:** Covers home building and contents; IRDAI's "Bharat Griha Raksha" covers fire, natural catastrophes, riots, terrorism, theft, etc.
- **Travel Insurance:** Covers emergency medical costs, trip cancellation, death/disability, luggage, and liability.
- **Endowment Policy:** Life insurance + maturity benefit; pays sum assured on both death and survival; premium costlier than term plans.
- **Unit Linked Insurance Policy (ULIP):** Combination of investment and protection; investment risk borne by policyholder.

### Government Insurance Schemes

**PMSBY (Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana)**
- One-year renewable Personal Accident Insurance for death/disability due to accident.
- Individuals (single/joint) aged 18–70 with participating bank/post office accounts, via auto-debit consent.
- Benefits: Death — ₹2 Lakh; total/irrecoverable loss of both eyes/hands/feet or one eye+one hand/foot — ₹2 Lakh; loss of sight of one eye or one hand/foot — ₹2 Lakh.
- Premium: ₹20/year per member. More: https://jansuraksha.gov.in/

**PMJAY (Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana / Ayushman Bharat)**
- Health cover of ₹5 lakh per family per year (family floater) for secondary/tertiary hospitalization.
- Cashless access at empanelled public/private hospitals.
- Covers 3 days pre-hospitalization and 15 days post-hospitalization expenses.
- No restriction on family size, age, gender; pre-existing conditions covered from day one; portable nationwide.
- More: https://pmjay.gov.in/

**PMJJBY (Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana)**
- One-year renewable term life insurance for death due to any cause.
- Age group 18–50, via auto-debit consent.
- ₹2 lakh payable on death; premium ₹436/year. More: https://jansuraksha.gov.in/

### General Instructions on Buying Insurance Products

1. **Purchase from Registered Insurance companies directly or through:** Registered Corporate/Individual Agents, Licensed Insurance Brokers, Licensed Web Aggregators, Registered CSCs, Registered Insurance Marketing Firms (IMF).
2. **Verify genuineness before payment:** Ask for ID proof, contact details (especially telesales), check IRDAI website, verify agents with insurer, pay only in the name of the insurance company (never an individual).
3. **Life Insurance Policy essentials:** Read the policy carefully, check premium mode/term/maturity benefits/lock-in/surrender value, ensure terms match the proposal; free-look period is 15 days for returns; pay premiums regularly; inform family/nominee about the policy.
4. **While purchasing insurance:** Read the prospectus/proposal carefully, fill details before signing, retain proposal copy, insurer must provide proposal form within 30 days, collect policy document promptly.
5. **Choose product suitable for your needs:** Based on life stage, financial position, purpose (savings, hospitalization, cover, pension, statutory needs), adequacy of sum assured, and tax benefits.

> **Do You Know?** IRDAI has launched standard products on life/health insurance like Arogya Sanjeevani and Corona Kavach. More: https://irdai.gov.in/standard-products-health-insurance

> **Beware!** Never fall prey to fictitious offers of high returns. IRDAI does not declare bonuses or sell insurance/financial products directly. Report unlicensed intermediaries to police (FIR) and IRDAI.

## 7. Retirement and Pension

After a full working life, we look forward to a healthy, active, secure retirement.

### Why is Retirement Planning Required?

- Rising Medical Expenses
- No pension
- Falling Interest Rates
- Increased Life Expectancy
- Plan for Spouse/children's future
- Protect post-retirement lifestyle

### Points to Keep in Mind for Retirement Planning

- Expected inflation during our lifetime
- Our life expectancy
- Expected age of retirement
- Period of accumulation and return on accumulated funds
- Post-retirement expected income and expenses

Inflation raises the cost of goods/services and simultaneously erodes the value of retirement savings. Increasing life expectancy requires higher provisioning post-retirement.

### National Pension System (NPS)

A defined contribution retirement scheme by Government of India, regulated by PFRDA. Voluntary, open to citizens aged 18–70. Minimum investment ₹500/year. At retirement (usually 60), part is withdrawn as lump sum and the rest invested in an annuity for monthly pension. Investments up to ₹2,00,000/year (as of Sept 2021) are income-tax exempt.

**How NPS Works:** Accumulation Phase (from current age to retirement/vesting age 60) → Maturity → Life-long Annuities (Withdrawal Phase). Up to 60% of corpus can be withdrawn; minimum 40% must go into an annuity plan.

**Exit Conditions:**
1. Conditional partial withdrawal up to 25% of contributions after 3 years of joining.
2. Exit before age 60/superannuation: Premature exit — 80% annuity purchase, 20% lump-sum.
3. Exit at 60/superannuation: Minimum 40% annuity purchase, maximum 60% lump-sum.

**Benefits of NPS:** Flexible, Simple and Tax Efficient, Portable, Regulated and Transparent, Dual benefit of Low Cost and Power of Compounding, Online Access.

More: https://www.pfrda.org.in | Pension calculator: http://www.npstrust.org.in/content/pension-calculator

### Atal Pension Yojana (APY)

For citizens aged 18–40 with a savings bank account, guaranteeing a minimum pension of ₹1,000–₹5,000/month from age 60. Contribution as low as ₹42/month at age 18 for ₹1,000 pension; contribution increases with joining age.

**3 Guaranteed Benefits of APY:**
1. Guaranteed minimum monthly pension (₹1,000–₹5,000) for life.
2. Same amount of lifelong pension for spouse after subscriber's death.
3. Nominee receives ₹1.70 Lakh to ₹8.50 Lakh after death of spouse.

On premature death (before 60), spouse can continue contributions or withdraw accumulated funds.

More: https://www.pfrda.org.in | Calculator: https://npstrust.org.in/apy-calculator

### Employee Provident Fund (EPF) Scheme

A retirement benefits scheme where employees contribute a small portion of basic pay monthly, matched by the employer. A social security scheme by EPFO providing pensions for organized-sector employees after retirement at age 58. More: https://www.epfindia.gov.in/

### Other Pension Plans

- **Pension Plans with Life Insurance Cover** (e.g., ULIP) — combination of life insurance and pension.
- **Pension Fund Oriented Hybrid Mutual Funds**
- **Immediate Annuity Plans** — provide annuity payment throughout life immediately after depositing the annuity fund amount.

### Retirement Planning

Retirement planning is the process of planning and managing short and long-term finances to achieve financial dreams during working years and retired life — analysing objectives, current position, and expected future cash flow.

**Benefits of Retirement Planning:** Maintains desired lifestyle in old age, plans for key life-stage events, provides financial security, ensures best use of hard-earned money post-retirement, covers contingencies.

**Key Life Events:** Buying a Home, Job Transition, Parenthood, Children's Education, Children's Marriage, Retirement Corpus.

### Sample Retirement Planning Example

**Assumptions:** Expected inflation during lifetime, life expectancy, expected rate of return post-retirement, expected retirement age, estimated current annual expenses of a retired couple. Typical career: start at 25, retire at 60; post-retirement life 60–80 years.

**Step 1 — Expected annual expenses in first year of retirement:**
Present age 30, present annual expenses ₹3.6 Lakh, inflation 7%, retirement at 60 (30 years away):
Expected annual expenses = 360000 × (1.07)^30 = **₹27,40,412**

**Step 2 — Real investment return post-retirement:**
Real Return = (Nominal Return − Inflation) / (1 + Inflation) = (8% − 7%) / 1.07 = **0.93%**

**Step 3 — Estimate the retirement corpus (life expectancy 80 → 20 post-retirement years):**
Using the time value of money equation with the ₹27,40,412 annual expense growing and a 0.93% real return over 20 years:
**Total corpus required ≈ ₹4,98,02,540**

So an individual with current annual expenses of ₹3,60,000, presently aged 30, needs a corpus of about **₹4.98 crore** at age 60 to maintain the same lifestyle 30 years later.

## 7. Consumer Protection and Grievance Redressal

Financial fraud/scam is a growing problem. Criminals constantly change tactics to find new victims. The first step in protection is knowing what fraud looks like.

### Types of Fraud or Scam

- **Ponzi Scheme:** Promises a return on investment each month; early investors get paid, encouraging more recruitment; scheme collapses when funding runs out.
- **Mass Marketing Fraud:** Fraudulent emails from fake "legitimate companies" leading to fake websites — never invest/donate/purchase by phone unless you can validate the company.
- **Investment Fraud:** Recruits you to invest in a business or sell merchandise, requiring you to recruit new members; promoters vanish once recruitment slows (pyramid-scheme style).
- **Lottery Scam:** "You've won! Just pay a small fee to claim." Legitimate contests never charge fees to collect prizes.
- **Credit and Debit Card Fraud:** Unauthorized use of card/PIN — never share your PIN.
- **Affinity Fraud:** Exploits trust within groups sharing a common cause (religious/social); scammers ask investors to keep quiet.

### Caveat Emptor ("Let the Buyer Beware")

The seller can be held responsible for failures only if a warranty/guarantee is given. Most financial products carry no guarantee. Always look for the fine-print symbol "*" in communications about fixed deposits, loans, high interest offers, product comparisons, etc.

- "Earn high interest rate* on savings account" — the "*" often means higher minimum balance is required.
- "Double your money in 10 years with 10%* return" — the "*" may reveal this is simple interest or "yield," not compound annual return.

### Data Security

- **Phishing:** Fraudulent emails from apparently reputable companies, inducing you to reveal passwords/card numbers online.
- **Hacking:** Breaking into computer systems/networks via bugs and other techniques.
- **Scamming:** Creating a fraudulent scheme to dupe innocent people.

> **Note:** No bank/financial organization requests detailed personal information, PINs, or passwords via email.

### If You Receive a Suspicious Email — Please Do:

- Do not reply.
- Do not open attachments (may contain malicious code).
- Do not click any links; do not enter confidential info if you accidentally click one.
- Do not copy-paste links into browsers — phishers disguise real-looking links.
- Use and keep updated anti-virus, anti-spyware, and a firewall.

### Please Do NOT:

- Click unknown/unverified links; delete such SMS/emails immediately.
- Enter your UPI PIN/password to *receive* money (receiving money never requires a PIN).
- Download apps from unverified/unknown sources or on a stranger's guidance.
- Write your ATM PIN on the card or enter it in front of strangers; never give your ATM card to anyone.
- Share SIM card identity credentials.
- Call numbers displayed directly on search engine result pages (often fraudster-camouflaged).
- Scan a QR code to *receive* money (receiving never requires scanning a QR/entering an m-PIN).
- Respond to messages promising prize money/government aid/KYC updates for prize claims.
- Make payments on unknown job search websites — genuine employers never charge for jobs.
- Respond to emails asking for bank account details or offering attractive commissions to route money for others.

For more on fraud precautions: https://rbidocs.rbi.org.in/rdocs/content/pdfs/BEAWARE07032022.pdf

**National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal Helpline: 1930 (24x7)** for immediate reporting of financial frauds.

### Grievance Redressal Channels

**1. Banking — RBI**
Approach your bank → Contact the Branch Manager → If no reply in 1 month, bank rejects complaint, or you're unsatisfied → Approach the Banking Ombudsman:
- Apply online: cms.rbi.org.in
- Apply in writing: Chief General Manager, Reserve Bank of India, Consumer Education & Protection Department, Central Office, 1st floor, Amar Building, Parim Nariman Street, Mumbai-400001
- No fee charged for filing/resolving complaints.

**2. Investment — SEBI**
Approach the concerned entity → if not satisfied → Approach SEBI:
- Lodge online complaint: http://scores.gov.in/ (SCORES – SEBI Complaints Redress System)
- Toll-free: 1800 266 7575 / 1800 22 7575
- https://scores.gov.in/scores/Welcome.html

**3. Insurance — IRDAI**
Fresh complaints via IRDA Grievance call center (paper/voice/email) or IGMS Portal (internet) → routed to Insurer → if no response or not satisfactory → escalate to IRDA → if still unsatisfactory → Ombudsman/Consumer Forum/Civil Court.
- Register online: www.igms.irda.gov.in
- Toll-free: 155255 or 1800 4254 732

**4. Pension — PFRDA**
Subscriber raises grievance via:
1. Web-based interface of CRA
2. Call centre/IVR of CRAs using T-pin
3. Physical form submission to concerned POP-SP

Routed through Central Grievance Management System (CGMS):
- Concerned Intermediary — Timeline 30 days (CGMS/Email/Written)
- NPS Trust — Timeline 30 days (CGMS/Email/Written)
- Ombudsman — Timeline 90 days (Written Application)
- Escalation → Designated Member of PFRDA — Timeline 60 days (Written) → Securities Appellate Tribunal (SAT)
