## 1. About the Handbook

This handbook is designed by the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority (IRDA) as a guide on Life Insurance Riders and gives general information only. Nothing in this handbook replaces or overrides the terms and conditions of an insurance policy or rider. For specific information regarding a rider, or any other additional information, approach a duly licensed agent or broker, or an insurance company registered with IRDA.

## 2. Life Insurance Riders

**Riders** are add-on options (benefits) that can be added to a basic Life Insurance policy to provide additional coverage. They help customize the coverage of a single insurance policy to a person's specific needs. The prospectus/promotional material for a policy normally indicates which riders can be attached to the basic product; a policyholder may add some or all of them for an extra premium per rider. Riders may be attached to either linked or non-linked life insurance policies.

Generally, riders must be opted for **at the time of taking the basic policy**, though some insurers may allow adding riders at a later date if explicitly stated in the promotional material.

### Common Riders

| Rider | Description |
|---|---|
| **Accidental Death / Permanent Disability Benefit Rider** | Covers the risk of death or permanent disability resulting from an accident. On accidental death, provides an additional benefit over and above the basic policy. On total/permanent disability due to accident, may provide benefits such as periodic payments, waiver of future premiums, etc. |
| **Critical Illness Rider** | Protects the insured on diagnosis of a critical illness specified at the outset of the contract. Pays an additional benefit as a lump sum or periodic payments, per the contract's terms. The insurer must specify the list of covered critical illnesses and exclusions. Most commonly covered illnesses: (i) Cancer, (ii) Heart attack, (iii) Kidney failure, (iv) Paralytic stroke, (v) Cardiac surgery. |
| **Guaranteed Insurability Rider** | Allows the insured to increase insurance cover per the policy's terms & conditions, without further medical examination, regardless of current health status. Useful for young individuals with currently low income who want to buy additional insurance as their income increases over time. |
| **Term Rider** | Pays an additional benefit on death (generally equal to the basic Sum Assured) over and above what the basic policy covers. |
| **Waiver of Premium Rider** | Waives future premiums if an insured contingency occurs (death, disability, etc.), allowing coverage to continue without financial strain. In some child plans, this rider waives future premiums if the covered parent dies prematurely. |
| **Spouse Insurance Rider** | Provides insurance coverage for the policyholder's spouse without requiring a separate insurance policy. |
| **Major Surgical Assistance Benefit** | Provides financial support for medical emergencies requiring surgery. Generally excludes pre-existing illnesses/injuries requiring surgery, and excludes hospitalization expenses for ailments that don't require surgery. |

> **Note:** The above list of riders is illustrative, not exhaustive. The scope of cover and benefits varies by rider and by insurer. Approach a duly licensed intermediary or insurer for specific information on a rider's availability.

## 3. Frequently Asked Questions on Riders

**Q: Why should I purchase a rider?**
Riders provide extra insurance cover/benefit on payment of additional premium without having to take out a separate policy. Different riders can be added to the basic policy according to individual needs and coverage requirements.

**Q: When do riders get terminated?**
Normally, a rider automatically terminates on discontinuance of the basic policy. A benefit rider may also terminate once its benefits are paid out, or upon occurrence of an insured event that ends further benefits under the rider.

**Q: Can a rider be offered/attached to every basic policy?**
Not necessarily — a rider can only be attached if the terms & conditions of the basic policy allow for it.

**Q: Are there restrictions on the coverage amount, premium, or number of riders?**
There is no restriction on the *number* of riders that can be added to a basic policy, provided they are offered under that policy. However:
- Premium for health-related or critical illness riders (for term or group products) **cannot exceed 100%** of the premium under the basic policy.
- All other riders combined are subject to a ceiling of **30%** of the basic policy's premium.
- Any benefit arising under a rider cannot exceed the sum assured under the basic policy.

**Q: At what time can I add a rider to my basic policy?**
Generally, insurers offer riders at the time of taking the basic policy, though some insurers allow adding riders at a later date.

**Q: What are the tax benefits under riders?**
- Premiums paid on riders (other than health and critical illness riders) qualify for tax benefits under **Section 80C**.
- Premiums for **Health** and **Critical Illness** riders qualify under **Section 80D**.
- All rider benefits received (claims) are exempt from tax under **Section 10(10D)**.
- Tax laws are subject to change and apply as per the laws prevailing on the date the insurer delivers the service.

**Q: Can I opt out of a rider I've added to my policy?**
Depending on the rider's terms, insurers may allow opting out of rider coverage. In that case, future premiums payable will equal the basic policy premium, excluding the rider portion.

**Q: Can I have a rider without a basic policy?**
No — a rider can only be offered as an add-on/additional benefit to a basic insurance policy, not as a standalone product.

## 4. Policyholder Servicing Turnaround Times (TATs) as Prescribed by IRDA

### General

| Service | Turnaround Time |
|---|---|
| Processing of proposal and communication of decisions, including requirements/issue of policy/cancellations | 15 days |
| Obtaining a copy of the proposal | 30 days |
| Post-policy-issue service requests concerning mistakes/refund of proposal deposit, and other non-claim-related service requests | 10 days |

### Life Insurance

| Service | Turnaround Time |
|---|---|
| Surrender value / annuity / pension processing | 10 days |
| Maturity claim / survival benefit / penal interest not paid | 15 days |
| Raising claim requirements after lodging the claim | 15 days |
| Death claim settlement without investigation requirement | 30 days |
| Death claim settlement/repudiation with investigation requirement | 6 months |

### General Insurance

| Service | Turnaround Time |
|---|---|
| Survey report submission | 30 days |
| Insurer seeking addendum report | 15 days |
| Settlement/rejection of claim after receiving first/addendum survey report | 30 days |

### Grievances

| Service | Turnaround Time |
|---|---|
| Acknowledge a grievance | 3 days |
| Resolve a grievance | 15 days |

## 5. If You Have a Grievance

The Consumer Affairs Department of IRDA operates the **Integrated Grievance Management System (IGMS)** — an online system for registering and tracking grievances.

**Process:**
1. Register your grievance first with the insurance company.
2. If unsatisfied with the company's resolution, escalate it to IRDA via IGMS at [www.igms.irda.gov.in](https://www.igms.irda.gov.in).
3. If you cannot access the insurer's grievance system directly, IGMS also provides a gateway to register your grievance with the insurer.

**Channels for grievance registration:**
- **Web:** IGMS ([www.igms.irda.gov.in](https://www.igms.irda.gov.in))
- **Email:** complaints@irda.gov.in
- **Letter:** Consumer Affairs Department, Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority, 3rd Floor, Parishram Bhavan, Basheerbagh, Hyderabad – 4
- **Toll-Free Call Centre:** 155255 (free of cost; registers complaints and helps track status)

**IRDA Call Centre details:**
- Assists by filling out the complaint form based on the caller's information.
- Facilitates filing complaints directly with insurance companies where required, providing the company's address, phone number, website, and email details.
- Operates as a 12-hours × 6-days service, **8 AM to 8 PM, Monday to Saturday**, in Hindi, English, and various Indian languages.

**Resolution process:** Once a complaint is registered with IRDA, IRDA facilitates resolution by taking it up with the insurance company, which is given **15 days** to resolve it. If required, IRDA carries out investigations and enquiries. Where applicable, IRDA advises the complainant to approach the **Insurance Ombudsman**, per the Redressal of Public Grievances Rules, 1998.
